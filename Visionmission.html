About Vision

The vision statement describes the ideal future or the long-term goal that the church aims to achieve. It should be inspiring and forward-looking, providing a sense of direction for the congregation. Some elements to consider in the vision statement:
  • Faith and Spirituality: Emphasize the church's commitment to deepening the faith of its members and fostering spiritual growth.
  • Community: Highlight the church's desire to build a strong, loving, and supportive community for its members and the broader society.
  • Impact: Express the church's aspiration to make a positive impact on individuals, families, and society as a whole through its activities and outreach.

About Mission

The mission statement outlines the church's primary purpose and the actions it will take to fulfill its vision. It should be concise and actionable, reflecting the church's unique identity and role in the community. Some elements to consider in the mission statement:
  • Worship and Teaching: Mention the importance of meaningful worship experiences and biblical teachings in the life of the church.
  • Service and Outreach: Highlight the commitment to serving others, both within the church family and the wider community.
  • Discipleship: Include the intention to disciple and equip believers to live out their faith in daily life.
